Sentiment Analysis for Political Discourse

F1 Macro: 78.17% 5-Class Classification BERTweet-large

Overview

Standard sentiment classifiers reduce social media commentary to a binary — positive or negative. This project expands that lens with a five-label schema designed to capture the actual character of political discourse online.

We fine-tuned BERTweet-large, a BERT variant pre-trained on Twitter data, to classify posts into one of five categories: Argumentative, Informational, Opinion, Expressive, or Neutral. Full label definitions are available in the repository and HuggingFace datacard. Evaluation used f1_macro to account for class imbalance in the dataset.

Key Findings

  • Final F1 macro on the holdout set was 78.17%.
  • The Opinion label was most commonly misclassified — predicted as Argumentative 15% of the time and Expressive 11% of the time, suggesting these label boundaries could be more cleanly defined in the schema.
  • BERTweet-large outperformed lighter BERT variants substantially, validating the use of domain-specific pretraining for social media text.

Use Cases

  • Political campaigns can use this model to understand the dominant discourse style on a platform and match the tone of their messaging to how people actually communicate there.
  • Political media and journalists can track how discourse type around a politician or news event shifts over time, informing stories and encouraging follow-up on past headlines.
  • Social media platforms can monitor how design choices affect discourse on their platform, adjusting recommendation systems and features in response.
